The Contextual Mentorship Flow

Storyboard: How the AI Rubber Duck cures Focus Debt in real-time.

The Risk: Focus Debt

She considers pinging Dave (Senior Engineer) on Slack. But Dave is in a "Flow State". If interrupted, Dr. Gloria Mark's research shows it will cost Dave 23 minutes and 15 seconds to recover his focus.

🦆 Scene 2: Engaging the Duck

Shift-Left Assistance

Instead of interrupting Dave, Sarah opens her IDE terminal and engages the AI Rubber Duck. She doesn't need complex prompt engineering; the environment already knows who she is and where she is.

🤖 Scene 4: System 2 Active Mentoring

Human Augmentation

The Duck responds. Unlike basic autocomplete tools that just spit out code, it acts like a Senior Engineer, citing sources and forcing Sarah to engage in analytical (System 2) thinking.

@Sarah

I see you're on RTR-842. According to Dave's recent ADR on state management (Source: GitHub MCP -> /docs/state.md), we enforce global context for checkout to prevent prop-drilling errors during PCI validation.

However, looking at your draft, you are making an API call directly in the component view.

Guardrail Alert: This violates the "No Network Hopping" rule.

How might you refactor this to move the side-effect into a dedicated service layer before we write the tests?
