# Product Requirements Document (PRD): AI Rubber Duck

## 1. Executive Summary & Product Vision
**Visionary Statement:** "The AI Rubber Duck is a **Visionary** in the *Contextual Mentorship* space, moving beyond basic 'Syntax Autocomplete' to become a proactive collaborative partner."

*   **The Mission:** To reclaim the "Focus Debt" of an organization by providing every developer with a context-aware mentor that scales with the company's knowledge.
*   **Competitive Differentiator:** While competitors (Cursor/Copilot) focus on *Velocity*, the AI Rubber Duck focuses on **Integrity**. It is the first "Shift-Left" assistant that audits for "Network Hopping" and enforces the **3 W's of Testing** (What, Where, When) during the development cycle.

## 2. Target Personas
| Persona | Role in Ecosystem | Pain Point | Success Metric |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Champion** (Mid-Level Engineer) | The "Active Builder" | Wants to avoid interrupting seniors for trivial architectural questions. | Reduction in "quick question" Slack pings. |
| **Secondary User** (Junior/Grad Engineer) | The "Learner" | Navigating a massive, unfamiliar codebase with complex internal patterns. | Faster Onboarding (Time-to-First-PR). |
| **Secondary User** (Vibe-Coding CTO) | The "Rapid Prototyper" | Needs high-level summaries of Jira progress and rapid context-switching. | High-quality, context-aware prototypes. |
| **Context Provider** (Senior Engineer) | The "Knowledge Source" | Tired of answering the same "How do we do X?" question 10 times. | Increase in focused, deep-work hours. |

## 3. Stack-Ranked KPIs
1.  **Senior Bandwidth Protection:** Percentage of questions deflected from senior engineers to the Duck.
2.  **Velocity (TTR):** Reduction in Time-to-Resolution for Jira tickets.
3.  **Shift-Left Quality:** Decrease in PR "bounce-backs" (rejections based on architectural or style violations).
4.  **Upskilling Excellence:** Measured by the complexity of tasks Junior engineers can complete without human intervention (aligned with AtlanTec judging).

## 4. Core Features & Architecture
### MVP: IDE Extension (Flow State Guardian)
*   **Active Listening Mode:** Proactively flags deviations from internal ADRs (Architectural Decision Records).
*   **Jira Integration (MCP):** Real-time summarization of the current ticket, its dependencies, and related historical tickets. 
*   **The "Audit" Trigger:** Automatically checks for "Network Hopping" in Unit/Component tests (FAIL immediately if found), enforcing RTR's 2025-2027 Quality Strategy.

### Secondary: CLI Interface (System Engineer Mode)
*   **Headless Context Retrieval:** Rapidly query organizational knowledge (Confluence/Jira) from the terminal.
*   **Automated PR Pre-Flight:** A `duck-check` command that validates the PR against the "3 W's" before it's pushed.

## 5. The "Context Flywheel" Moat (RTR_MCP)
Our unique advantage is the **RTR_MCP (Model Context Protocol)**. This creates an organizational flywheel:
1.  **Incentivized Documentation:** Seniors write docs to "train" their shield (the Duck).
2.  **Instant Indexing:** The Duck instantly consumes these docs via MCP.
3.  **Deflected Interruptions:** Future junior questions are handled by the Duck.
4.  **Time Reclaimed:** Seniors gain back time, allowing them to write *better* documentation.
